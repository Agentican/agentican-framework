# Research: Top 3 Large Language Models for Reasoning and Tool Use

Based on recent benchmarks and evaluations (2024-2026), here are the top three large language models that excel at reasoning and tool use capabilities:

## 1. **OpenAI o3 (and o4-mini)**

**Organization:** OpenAI
**Release Date:** January 31, 2025 (o3-mini), April 16, 2025 (o3 and o4-mini)
**Current Status:** Generally available via ChatGPT Plus/Pro/Team, API, and enterprise plans

### Key Strengths in Reasoning

OpenAI o3 scored 96.7% on the American Invitational Mathematics Exam and 87.7% on a graduate-level science exam (GPQA Diamond), highlighting the model's increased capacity for solving complex problems in fields that require high-level reasoning. On the Frontier Math Benchmark, o3 recorded around 25% accuracy compared to the previous best of 2%, showcasing its superior mathematical reasoning abilities, and scored an impressive 96.7% accuracy on AIME, missing only one question.

On the ARC-AGI benchmark, which evaluates an AI's ability to handle new logical and skill acquisition problems, o3 attained three times the accuracy of o1. With 87.5% accuracy in visual reasoning on ARC-AGI, o3 addresses prior models' struggles with spatial and physical object analysis.

### Tool Use Capabilities

For the first time, OpenAI's reasoning models can agentically use and combine every tool within ChatGPT—this includes searching the web, analyzing uploaded files and other data with Python, reasoning deeply about visual inputs, and even generating images. Both o3 and o4-mini were trained to use tools through reinforcement learning—teaching them not just how to use tools, but to reason about when to use them.

### Notable Benchmark Scores

- **AIME 2024:** 96.7% (compared to o1's 83.3%)
- **GPQA Diamond:** 87.7%
- **ARC-AGI (high-compute):** 87.5%
- **Frontier Math:** 25.2% (previous best <2%)
- **SWE-bench Verified:** 71.7%
- **Codeforces:** Elo rating of 2727

### Performance Notes

At equal latency and cost with OpenAI o1, o3 delivers higher performance in ChatGPT—and they've validated that if they let it think longer, its performance keeps climbing. o3 now surpasses o1 across nearly every benchmark, with full tool access in ChatGPT and via API.

## 3. **Google Gemini 3 Pro (and Deep Think)**

**Organization:** Google DeepMind
**Release Date:** November 18, 2025
**Current Status:** Available via Gemini app, Google AI Studio, Vertex AI; Deep Think mode in limited release

### Key Strengths in Reasoning

Gemini 3 Pro demonstrates PhD-level reasoning with top scores on Humanity's Last Exam (37.5% without the usage of any tools) and GPQA Diamond (91.9%). It also sets a new standard for frontier models in mathematics, achieving a new state-of-the-art of 23.4% on MathArena Apex.

Gemini 3 Pro's 31.1% score on ARC-AGI-2 (45.1% with Deep Think) is a massive jump from Gemini 2.5 Pro (4.9%) and nearly doubles the score of GPT-5.1 (17.6%). Most importantly this is the first time we're seeing a 40%+ result with Gemini 3 deep think, and 37.5% with Gemini 3 Pro, on the hardest reasoning test, the Humanity's Last Exam.

### Tool Use and Agentic Capabilities

Gemini 3.1 brings exceptional instruction following – with meaningful improved tool use and agentic coding. Gemini 3.1's agentic capabilities can build more helpful and intelligent personal AI assistants.

Gemini 3 Pro's result on ScreenSpot-Pro, a key benchmark for agentic computer use, rose from 11.4% to 72.7%, demonstrating exceptional capability in UI interaction and tool manipulation.

### Notable Benchmark Scores

## Summary

All three leading models demonstrate exceptional reasoning capabilities with distinct advantages:

- **OpenAI o3** leads in pure mathematical reasoning and abstract problem-solving with the highest AIME and Frontier Math scores
- **Claude Opus 4.5** dominates in real-world software engineering tasks, tool use, and sustained multi-step workflows
- **Gemini 3 Pro** excels in multimodal reasoning, scientific knowledge (GPQA), and offers the most comprehensive agentic capabilities with superior tool integration

The choice between these models depends on specific use cases: o3 for mathematical/logical reasoning, Claude for coding and long-horizon tasks, and Gemini for multimodal understanding and integrated tool use.
