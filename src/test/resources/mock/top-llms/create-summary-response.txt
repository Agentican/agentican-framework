# Top 3 LLMs for Reasoning and Tool Use: Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive benchmark evaluations through early 2025, three models stand out for exceptional reasoning and tool use capabilities. Each excels in distinct domains while delivering state-of-the-art performance across multiple benchmarks.

**Quick Rankings:**
1. **OpenAI o3** - Best for mathematical and abstract reasoning
2. **Anthropic Claude Opus 4.5** - Best for software engineering and long-horizon tasks
3. **Google Gemini 3 Pro** - Best for multimodal reasoning and integrated tool use

---

## 1. OpenAI o3 (and o4-mini)

**Organization:** OpenAI
**Status:** Generally available (January-April 2025)

### Key Strengths

**Mathematical & Abstract Reasoning Excellence**
- Achieved 96.7% on AIME 2024 (American Invitational Mathematics Exam), missing just one question
- Scored 25.2% on Frontier Math benchmark—more than 12x the previous best of <2%
- Attained 87.5% on ARC-AGI visual reasoning, triple the accuracy of its predecessor o1
- Earned Elo rating of 2727 on Codeforces competitive programming

**Advanced Tool Integration**
- First OpenAI reasoning model to agentically use and combine every ChatGPT tool
- Trained via reinforcement learning to reason about when to use tools, not just how
- Can simultaneously search the web, analyze files with Python, process visual inputs, and generate images

**Why It Ranks #1:** OpenAI o3 delivers unmatched performance on pure reasoning tasks, especially mathematics and logic. Its 96.7% AIME score and breakthrough Frontier Math results (25.2% vs. <2% previously) represent the strongest mathematical reasoning capabilities available. The model's performance scales with increased thinking time, making it ideal for complex problem-solving.

---

## 2. Anthropic Claude Opus 4.5

**Organization:** Anthropic
**Status:** Available via Claude Pro and API (November 2025+)

### Key Strengths

**Software Engineering Dominance**
- First model to exceed 80% on SWE-bench Verified with 80.9% accuracy
- 65% improvement over Claude 3.5 Sonnet and outperforms all competitors on real bug resolution
- Leads across 7 out of 8 programming languages on SWE-bench Multilingual
- Achieved 89.4% on Aider Polyglot coding benchmark

**Long-Horizon Task Execution**
- Can autonomously sustain complex, multi-step tasks for over 30 hours
- Scored 37.6% on ARC-AGI-2—more than double GPT-5.1's 17.6%
- Excels at maintaining coherent step-by-step plans over thousands of tokens
- "Best frontier task planning and tool calling we've seen yet" according to early testers

**Why It Ranks #2:** Claude Opus 4.5 is the undisputed leader for real-world software engineering and coding tasks. Its record-breaking SWE-bench Verified score (80.9%) and ability to maintain complex workflows over extended periods make it the top choice for agentic systems, software development, and tasks requiring sustained reasoning across multiple steps.

---

## 3. Google Gemini 3 Pro (with Deep Think)

**Organization:** Google DeepMind
**Status:** Available via Gemini app, AI Studio, Vertex AI (November 2025)

### Key Strengths

**PhD-Level Scientific Reasoning**
- Achieved 91.9% on GPQA Diamond (93.8% with Deep Think mode)
- Scored 37.5% on Humanity's Last Exam (41% with Deep Think)
- Perfect 100% on AIME 2025 with code execution, 95% without tools
- First model to break 1500 Elo on LMArena (scored 1501)

**Multimodal Reasoning Leadership**
- Scored 81% on MMMU-Pro and 87.6% on Video-MMMU, redefining multimodal reasoning
- Achieved 72.7% on ScreenSpot-Pro (up from 11.4%), demonstrating exceptional UI interaction capabilities
- Delivered 72.1% on SimpleQA Verified for factual accuracy

**Why It Ranks #3:** Gemini 3 Pro offers the most comprehensive and balanced capabilities, excelling across scientific reasoning, multimodal understanding, and agentic tool use. Its PhD-level performance on GPQA Diamond (91.9%) and exceptional video understanding make it the strongest choice for applications requiring diverse reasoning across text, images, and video, as well as integrated tool manipulation.

## Important Considerations

**Cost & Latency:** These frontier models require significant compute. OpenAI o3's performance improves with longer thinking time, which impacts both cost and response latency. Consider use case requirements when selecting.

**Access & Availability:** All three models are available through their respective platforms (ChatGPT, Claude Pro, Gemini), though specific features like Deep Think mode may have limited availability.

**Open Source Alternative:** DeepSeek-R1 (MIT license, 671B parameters) offers comparable reasoning performance at significantly lower cost, achieving 87.5% on AIME 2025—a viable option for those prioritizing cost-efficiency and open access.

**Task-Specific Performance:** While all three excel at reasoning and tool use, no single model dominates across all benchmarks. Selection should align with specific needs:
- Choose **o3** for mathematical proofs, competitive programming, or abstract logic
- Choose **Claude Opus 4.5** for autonomous coding agents, bug fixes, or sustained multi-step workflows
- Choose **Gemini 3 Pro** for scientific research, multimodal analysis, or comprehensive agentic systems

---

## Recommendation

**For most reasoning-intensive applications requiring tool use:** Start with **Claude Opus 4.5** for its balanced performance, exceptional software engineering capabilities, and proven long-horizon execution. Escalate to **OpenAI o3** when facing mathematically complex problems, or choose **Gemini 3 Pro** when multimodal understanding is critical to your workflow.

All three models represent the current state-of-the-art and demonstrate that frontier AI has reached unprecedented levels of reasoning sophistication and tool integration capabilities.
